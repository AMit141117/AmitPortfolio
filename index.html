Who I Am

I'm a cybersecurity postgraduate specializing in Digital Forensics and Incident Response (DFIR), currently pursuing my MSc at Lok Jagruti Kendra University, Ahmedabad.

My journey into cybersecurity is driven by a passion for uncovering the truth hidden in digital evidence — whether that's tracing a cybercriminal through file system artifacts, analyzing malware behavior, or responding to active incidents.

I've had the privilege of working at the Directorate of Forensic Science (DFS), Gandhinagar — a government law enforcement forensic laboratory — where I perform real forensic investigations on seized devices. Previously, I interned at RFSL, Surat, contributing to cybercrime case analysis.

I built ShadowMorph 2.0, a controlled malware simulation, to understand attacker tradecraft from the inside — how malware persists, evades detection, and communicates — so I can build better defenses.
